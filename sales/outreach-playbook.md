## Who buys this

The best buyers are not random token communities. They are teams that make decisions about liquidity, incentives, growth, or ecosystem reporting.

Buyer personas:
- DEX operators that need to understand volume, fees, weak pairs, and market quality.
- Protocol BD/growth teams that need an external evidence pack before partner conversations.
- Ecosystem teams that need source-backed reporting for OP/Base/Mode/Unichain liquidity programs.
- DAO contributors who need a short decision memo before discussing incentives.
- Yield and lending protocols that need proof around deposits, liquidity depth, and market activity.

Do not pitch this as "a dashboard subscription" at first. Pitch it as a finished operator report that saves them time and gives them something they can forward internally.

## Why they would pay

Strong sales reasons:
- They already have public data, but it is scattered across dashboards.
- They need a short source-backed explanation, not another tab to check.
- Liquidity programs are expensive; a $500 report is cheap if it helps avoid a bad incentive decision.
- A report gives BD, growth, and DAO contributors a concrete artifact to discuss.
- The first report can become recurring monitoring if they like the format.

The strongest pain angles:
- "Your fee output looks light relative to visible volume."
- "Your 7d volume moved sharply and needs a clean explanation."
- "Your Superchain footprint is spread across chains and needs one view."
- "Your pool activity is visible, but not packaged into a decision memo."

## Outreach workflow

1. Open the lead in `sales/lead-targets-30.csv`.
2. Verify official X, Discord, docs, or contact page manually before sending.
3. Send a short X/Telegram/Discord DM first.
4. If there is no reply after 48 hours, send the email version if a public team email/contact form exists.
5. If there is no reply after 3-4 days, send follow-up 1.
6. If there is no reply after 7 days, send follow-up 2 and move the lead to "cold".
7. If they reply, do not over-explain. Offer a one-page sample scope and ask which market/chain they care about most.

CRM fields to track:
- Protocol
- Contact route
- Date contacted
- Message version
- Response
- Next action date
- Status: new, contacted, replied, scope sent, paid, cold
- Notes

## Reply scripts

If they say "interesting":

```text
Great. Which market or chain should I focus on first?

I can send a one-page scope with:
1. the exact pools/markets covered,
2. source links,
3. delivery date,
4. fixed price in OP/USDT/USDC.
```

If they ask for price:

```text
The first paid pilot is $500 fixed. Standard reports after that are $1,500, and recurring monitoring starts at $750/mo.

For the pilot I usually do 50% upfront and 50% before final delivery, paid in OP, USDT, or USDC.
```

If they say "no budget":

```text
Understood. The lowest useful version is a narrow $500 report for one chain or one weak market. If that is still not a fit, I can keep you on the monitoring list and reach back out only if a clear liquidity signal appears.
```

If they ask "why should we trust you":

```text
Fair question. The workflow is public-source only: all claims link back to source data, and the output includes CSV evidence so your team can verify the numbers.

I can start with a one-page scope before any payment so you can judge whether the report would be useful.
```

If they ask for a free report:

```text
I can send a short sample scope for free, but the actual protocol-specific report is paid because it includes manual review, source checks, CSV evidence, and written recommendations.

The smallest starting point is the $500 pilot.
```

If they say "wrong person":

```text
Thanks. Who is the best person for liquidity, growth, or ecosystem reporting?

I can keep the message short and send only the sample scope.
```

## Sales rules

- Do not send more than 10 first-touch messages per day from a new account.
- Do not send identical copy to every team; always change the chain and pain angle.
- Do not say "we found a problem" in the first line.
- Do not promise returns, TVL growth, or incentive success.
- Do not claim private client traction that does not exist.
- Do not send private DMs to random employees if the project has an official public route.
- Keep the first goal small: get permission to send a sample scope.

## Closing checklist

Before taking payment:
- Confirm protocol name.
- Confirm chain/market scope.
- Confirm price and token.
- Confirm wallet/network for payment.
- Confirm delivery date.
- Confirm whether the report can be used as a public sample after delivery.

Before delivery:
- Re-run public source checks.
- Export PDF.
- Export CSV evidence.
- Add source links.
- Add a short action summary.
- Remove internal CRM notes.
